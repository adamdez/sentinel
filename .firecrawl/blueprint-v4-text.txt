 SENTINEL BUILD BLUEPRINT Fourth Revision — Forward-Looking Engineering Plan Dominion Home Deals Operator-first CRM · Intelligence Layer · Conversation Workspace · Agent Fleet · MCP Mesh March 19, 2026 | Fourth Revision Internal working blueprint for the Sentinel engineer Prepared with 2026–2027 AI tooling landscape in view This revision integrates the stacked prospecting audit, prospect engine bake-off strategy, expanded provider landscape, competitive intelligence layer, agent tooling gap analysis, and 8 forward-looking corrections from the March 2026 tool ecosystem review. 1. Executive Directive The one-sentence product thesis: Sentinel should become a deterministic, auditable, operator-first acquisitions and dispo operating system powered by a fleet of domain-specific AI agents coordinated through an MCP mesh, a traced intelligence layer, and a first-class conversation workspace — removing research and admin labor without eroding trust or workflow clarity. 1.1 Main Goal The main goal is not to build a clever AI CRM. The main goal is to build a two-person operating machine that lets Dominion Home Deals generate roughly $2 million per year without adding support labor at the same rate. The software matters only because it compresses execution, context, and confidence into a tighter operating loop. Every engineering decision, every AI feature, every tooling choice should be tested against one question: does this increase contracts per founder-hour enough to justify the complexity and risk? 1.2 The 2026 Leverage Thesis The AI tooling landscape has shifted fundamentally since the original blueprint was written. The correct mental model is no longer “add AI features to a CRM.” The correct mental model is “build the CRM as the deterministic action core, then surround it with a fleet of AI agents that handle research, follow-up, alerting, QA, and dispo — all coordinated through MCP and a workflow engine, with humans reviewing and deciding at high-stakes moments.” Evidence that this works in production right now: Jason Lemkin (SaaStr) replaced his entire sales team with 20 AI agents managed by 1.2 humans. AI emails were better than human-written. 10x volume. 15% of event revenue. Alex Shartsis built a Claude + MCP personal CRM in 20 minutes for $20/mo that delivers 90%+ of traditional CRM value for follow-up management. Jacob Bank (Relay.app) runs his entire marketing org as himself + 40 AI agents doing the work of a 5-person team. MCP has 97 million monthly SDK downloads. Over 100 specialized Claude Code subagent templates are open-sourced on GitHub. Sentinel should be built to leverage this reality, not to ignore it. 1.3 The Stacked Prospecting Thesis (New in v4) The RE investor tool landscape has converged around full-stack operating platforms. PropStream now bundles search → skip trace → dial → campaign execution. BatchLeads pushes BatchRank-AI with built-in dialer and mobile field workflows. DealMachine bundles Alma AI with satellite-image-based lead lists. RealFlow pitches an engine that detects likely sellers, runs outreach, and books qualified meetings. This means the market is no longer “list provider over here, dialer over there, CRM over there.” It is shifting toward full investor operating lanes. Sentinel’s correct response is not to compete with these platforms at list generation. It is to become the operating membrane that ingests from multiple live cohort engines, measures each against actual contract output, and owns the durable working truth, promotion logic, seller memory, and exception handling that no off-the-shelf platform can replicate. The rule: stop thinking in terms of one feed. Sentinel should ingest from multiple prospect engines and measure them against contracts, not lead volume. 1.4 What Success Looks Like Logan can open any serious lead and know what to do in under a minute. Every important lead has a next action, a reason, and a fallback if the next action fails. Property context is available inside Sentinel without bouncing between XLeads, Zillow, county sites, and spreadsheets. Offers move faster because comps, valuation state, confidence, and decision support are already staged. Dispo moves faster because buyer-fit and buyer memory are visible early. Missed calls, callback failures, stale follow-up, and offer stalls become visible exceptions instead of silent losses. AI reduces research, note-taking, QA, and routing labor without replacing Logan in trust-heavy human moments. The dialer is a first-class conversation workspace where Logan’s calls are better prepared, better captured, and better followed up. A fleet of domain-specific AI agents handles research, follow-up drafting, exception monitoring, QA, and dispo preparation — with operator review gates at every trust-sensitive moment. An AI receptionist answers every inbound call within seconds, 24/7. Multiple prospect engines feed Sentinel, each measured against cost-per-contract, not just lead volume. 1.5 What Success Is Not Not a broad investor ERP with dozens of half-used modules. Not a giant universal MCP shell that talks to everything but owns nothing clearly. Not a no-code automation nest where business rules are scattered across external workflow tools. Not a research toy that is smarter in analysis than in execution. Not an autonomous seller-negotiation system. Not a system that builds everything custom when a $49/mo product already solves the problem. Not a platform that competes with PropStream or BatchLeads at list generation instead of using them as inputs. 2. Non-Negotiable Principles and Anti-Goals 2.1 Build Principles Sentinel remains the system of action. Leads, stages, tasks, calls, offers, dispo states, and operator-facing summaries stay in Sentinel. The intelligence layer is the system of understanding. Raw imports, research artifacts, extracted facts, dossiers, confidence logic, contradiction logic, provider mapping, review, and traces stay outside the action core. The dialer is the system of conversation. Live call state, context injection, seller memory, AI notes, transcript handling, disposition capture, post-call review, and coaching belong to the dialer workspace, not the master client file. Same repo, same database first. The product should ship as a bounded monolith before any physical service split. Revenue-impacting features first. Sequence the build by what makes Logan’s next call better, not by what makes the architecture prettier. Buy, integrate, or build — in that order. For every capability, ask: does a product already exist that solves this for &lt;$200/mo? If yes, buy it. Does an API or platform exist that can be wired in? If yes, integrate it. Only build custom when the capability is your competitive moat or no product exists. Direct provider adapters first; MCP where it helps. For PropertyRadar and ATTOM, direct REST adapters are the stable path. No durable CRM writeback from untraced runs. Every AI-assisted workflow that can change CRM state must have run IDs, prompt/version identity, inputs, outputs, and review policy. Evidence before inference. Facts tie back to artifacts, dossiers summarize the best facts, and projections summarize the dossier. Build slices, not platforms. Each phase ships a vertical slice from intake to review to CRM projection. Think forward, not backward. Every tool, platform, and capability decision should be evaluated against what exists in 2026 and what is coming in 2027 — not against what was available when the original plan was written. Measure prospect engines against contracts, not leads. Multiple list/discovery sources feed Sentinel. Each is measured by cost-per-contract and qualified conversation rate, not by records surfaced. 2.2 Anti-Goals Do not rebuild Sentinel from scratch unless the current codebase is beyond salvage after audit. Do not let provider-specific field names leak into Sentinel tables or UI text. Do not place core lead-routing or offer logic inside n8n, Zapier, or ad hoc scripts. Do not auto-skip-trace every property lookup. Trace only on promotion to a real working lead. Do not build full autonomous cold AI calling as a core production lane before compliance controls and session ownership are strong. Do not grow the dialer feature inside the master client file modal. Do not build custom what you can buy for &lt;$200/mo — unless it is your competitive moat. Do not evaluate tools based on whether you have used them before. Evaluate based on whether they serve the main goal. Do not confuse a safeguard with a goal, a feature with a business outcome, or activity with leverage. Do not compete with PropStream, BatchLeads, or DealMachine at list generation. Use them as inputs. Compete at operating intelligence, conversation quality, and deal throughput. 3. End-State Product Definition 3.1 What Sentinel Should Feel Like Universal address search from the main shell. Any serious property can become a working lead without leaving Sentinel. Property card shows operator-grade facts: address, owner clues, mortgage/equity, transaction history, listing status, comp state, condition signals, warnings. Lead record is lean. Deep evidence is one click away in the dossier. Dialer is a first-class conversation workspace with pre-call context, seller memory, live AI notes, transcript, objection tagging, disposition, and publish-to-client-file flow. Dispo is not an afterthought. Buyer-fit, buyer memory, outreach state, and stale-dispo detection are visible. Every morning, Logan opens Sentinel and sees: top 10 priority leads, overdue follow-ups, yesterday’s missed opportunities, today’s callbacks. The exception agent has already surfaced what matters. AI agents handle research enrichment, follow-up drafting, exception monitoring, and QA in the background. Operators review and approve at trust-sensitive moments. Multiple prospect engines feed qualified records into Sentinel. Source/market attribution tracks which engine produces contracts, not just which one produces leads. 3.2 The Three-Workspace Boundary The master client file is for durable truth. The dialer is for active conversation. The only thing that moves from dialer to file is curated outcome data. Master Client File (Durable Truth) Canonical property + owner record, timeline, distress signals, comps, relationship history, outcomes. Receives only finalized, operator-reviewed outputs from the dialer. Dialer Workspace (Active Conversation) Live transcript, notes, AI assist, objection prompts, script guidance, disposition capture. This surface grows as the dialer gets smarter. Consumes a compact context snapshot from the CRM. Shared Context Snapshot (Read-Only Bridge) Lightweight CRM context for the dialer: owner name, address, summary, distress flags, tasks, prior calls, objections, offer posture, timeline, DNC flags. 3.3 The Agentic Operating Layer Behind the three workspaces, a fleet of domain-specific AI agents operates continuously. Each agent is narrow, specialized, review-gated, and traceable. Agents share context through the Sentinel MCP server and are coordinated by the workflow engine. Research Agent: Intelligence domain. Triggered by lead promotion or operator request. Produces enriched property facts, dossier draft, contradiction flags. Review console before CRM sync. Follow-Up Agent: Action core. Triggered by stale lead or operator-scheduled. Produces personalized follow-up draft using seller memory. Operator approval before send. Exception Agent: Action core. Nightly scan + real-time SLA monitors. Produces morning priority brief, exception alerts via n8n. Informational — no write. QA Agent: Dialer. Post-call summary published. Produces quality rating, missed-mirror flags, talk-ratio analysis. Informational — flags only. Dispo Agent: Action core. Deal goes under contract. Produces ranked buyer-fit list, outreach drafts. Operator selects buyer and approves outreach. Ads Monitor Agent: Ads. Daily cron + anomaly threshold. Produces performance alerts, waste detection, budget warnings. Informational — recommendations require approval. 4. Architecture Blueprint 4.1 Domain Map Action core (Sentinel): Owns leads, stages, tasks, notes, calls, offers, dispo, source/market attribution, projections. Does not own raw provider payloads, AI traces, research artifacts, model logic. Rule: Fast operator truth only. Dialer workspace: Owns call sessions, live notes, transcripts, AI suggestions, disposition, seller memory, extracted facts before confirmation. Does not own permanent lead/property state, CRM workflow rules. Rule: Volatile conversation state. Publish curated outputs only. Intelligence layer: Owns artifacts, fact assertions, dossiers, provider normalization, review queue, buyer-fit, contradiction flags. Does not own direct operator workflow state. Rule: Understand first; publish lean outputs. Control plane / runtime: Owns run IDs, prompt versions, tool logs, eval datasets, approvals, rollout flags, event flows, feature flags. Does not own permanent business truth by itself. Rule: Nothing durable writes without traceability. Agent fleet: Owns domain-specific agents (research, follow-up, exception, QA, dispo, ads), MCP tool access, workflow coordination. Does not own direct CRM writes without review gates. Rule: Agents propose; operators approve. Provider adapters: Owns API auth, mapping, retries, provenance, rate-limit handling. Does not own business decisions and CRM rules. Rule: Convert provider shapes into canonical fields. Operational glue (n8n): Owns notifications, alerts, delivery, webhook fan-out, calendar glue, non-core automation. Does not own core CRM logic, scoring, offer decisions, lead routing. Rule: Deliver, don’t decide. 4.2 Hard Write-Path Rule Provider payload → raw record / artifact → normalized fact assertions → dossier / assessment → review policy → CRM sync snapshot → Sentinel projection. No provider payload writes directly into lead tables. No model output writes directly without a review or policy gate. 4.3 Dialer Write-Path Rule Live call data stays in dialer-session storage. Final summary generated at call end. Only selected structured facts promoted to client file. Call session data is volatile. Client file data is curated. 4.4 Agent Write-Path Rule Agents read from the Sentinel MCP server and external MCP servers (Twilio, Playwright, etc.). Agents write proposals to review queues or draft tables. Operator-approved proposals are promoted to durable CRM state through the standard write path. No agent writes directly to lead, deal, or calls_log tables without passing through a review or policy gate. 4.5 MCP Mesh Architecture All AI systems (agents, voice AI, Claude Code, n8n AI nodes) interact with Sentinel through a standardized MCP interface. This means you build the MCP server once and every new AI tool you adopt can immediately talk to your CRM. Sentinel MCP (custom, Build Phase 1–2): Exposes lead lookup, property context, call outcomes, task creation, follow-up scheduling, pipeline queries. Used by all agents, voice AI, Claude Code, n8n. Supabase MCP (production, connected): Database queries, migrations, Edge Functions, branching. Used by Claude Code (dev time). Google Ads MCP (production, official): Campaign queries, performance data, natural language ad analysis. Used by Ads Monitor Agent, Claude Code. Twilio MCP (alpha, 1,700+ endpoints): Call management, SMS, phone numbers, recordings, status. Used by Follow-Up Agent, voice platform. Slack MCP (GA, Feb 2026): Pipeline alerts, daily summaries, team notifications. Used by Exception Agent via n8n. Playwright MCP (production, Microsoft): Browser automation, web scraping, form filling, data extraction. Used by Research Agent. Google Calendar MCP (community): Callback scheduling, appointment management. Used by Voice AI, Follow-Up Agent. Composio MCP (evaluate Phase 3): 250+ pre-built tool integrations for AI agents. Could reduce custom integration work for agent fleet connections to Gmail, Calendar, Slack, and other services. Evaluate as complement to Claude Agent SDK. 4.6 Internal Module Map sentinel-core: leads, stages, tasks, notes, calls, offers, dispo states, UI projections. dialer-core: call sessions, live notes, transcripts, AI suggestions, seller memory, context snapshot, publish-to-CRM. intel-core: artifacts, facts, dossiers, buyer-fit, contradiction flags, research jobs. runtime-core: run registry, prompt registry, approvals, evaluations, event logging, feature flags. agent-core: agent definitions, MCP tool registry, workflow coordination, review queue integration. mcp-server: Sentinel MCP server exposing lead/property/call/task tools to all AI systems. providers/*: propertyradar, attom, mls, skiptrace, voice, browser, bricked, regrid. 5. Sales Philosophy and AI Coaching Framework Every AI-assisted script, coaching prompt, objection handler, and call QA evaluation inside Sentinel should be grounded in coherent sales philosophy. These principles draw from NEPQ (Jeremy Miner / 7th Level), Chris Voss’s tactical empathy (Never Split the Difference), and common-sense adaptations for distressed real estate sellers. 7.1 Core Sales Principles From NEPQ: Question-Led Discovery The seller discovers their own problem. Do not push solutions. Use systematic questioning. AI coaching should prompt toward problem-awareness questions, not pitch scripts. Connection before situation. NEPQ phases: connection (build trust), situation (understand state), problem-awareness (surface pain), solution-awareness (what solving looks like), consequence (cost of inaction), commitment (next steps). AI scripts follow this arc. The seller persuades themselves. AI coaching flags when Logan is talking too much and not asking enough. From Chris Voss: Tactical Empathy The adversary is the situation, not the seller. Frame calls as collaborative problem-solving. Mirroring: Repeat the last 1–3 key words with curious intonation. Then stay silent. AI coaching detects missed mirroring opportunities. Labeling: Name the seller’s emotion. ‘It sounds like this has been weighing on you.’ The AI objection library includes label-based responses, not just rebuttals. Accusation audit: Name the seller’s biggest fear before they voice it. Disarms resistance before it forms. Calibrated questions: ‘How’ and ‘what’ questions put problem-solving on the seller. Get to ‘That’s right.’ AI should detect whether Logan is getting ‘That’s right’ (deeply understood) vs ‘You’re right’ (they want you to stop talking). Common-Sense for Distressed Real Estate Never sound like a call center. Tone must be local, patient, genuine. Be ready for ‘How did you get my number?’ Standardized, clean, honest answer. Do not use inferred family details in outreach. Internal knowledge is for preparation, not conversation openers. Price is not the first conversation. AI coaching flags premature price discussion. Follow-up is where deals close. Most sellers need 3–10 touches. System makes follow-up effortless. 7.2 Feature Mapping Pre-call brief: Gives Logan facts to ask better questions, not pitch. Seller memory: Enables continuity: reference prior objections, follow-up, timeline. Live AI notes: Captures seller words so Logan mirrors and labels more effectively. Objection library: Labels and calibrated questions, not scripted rebuttals. Call QA Agent: Talk-listen ratio, question quality, missed mirrors, premature price. Post-call summary: Extracts seller-spoken motivation, timeline, objections. Follow-Up Agent: References specific seller statements, not generic templates. AI receptionist: Connection questions, labels caller emotion, books callback with context. Synthetic seller lab: Practice NEPQ and Voss techniques against simulated sellers. 7.3 Anti-Patterns Do not optimize for talk time or aggressive closing. Optimize for question quality and follow-up discipline. Do not generate manipulative language. Help Logan be prepared, empathetic, structured. Do not let AI scripts become robotic. Every script is a starting point Logan adapts. 8.

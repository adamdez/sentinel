# Tina Research Policy

Last updated: 2026-03-26

This document is the durable rulebook for how Tina should research tax ideas.

## Core Rule

Tina may search broadly for ideas, but she may file narrowly.

That means:

- broad discovery is allowed
- direct filing support must come from primary authority
- anything close to the edge must be reviewed by a human

## Two-Lane Research Model

### 1. Discovery Lane

Purpose:

- find ideas
- surface missed deductions
- notice elections or structures the owner may not know about
- collect industry-specific and edge-case leads

Allowed source classes:

- primary authority
- secondary analysis
- community leads
- low-trust leads

Important rule:

- discovery sources do not become filing support just because they sound clever

### 2. Authority Lane

Purpose:

- prove or kill a tax idea
- determine whether the idea is usable
- determine whether disclosure is needed
- determine whether the idea must be rejected

Allowed source classes for final support:

- primary authority only

Examples of primary authority:

- IRS
- Washington Department of Revenue
- Treasury regulations
- statutes
- official court opinions
- official IRS instructions and bulletins

## Source Classes

### Primary authority

Examples:

- `irs.gov`
- `dor.wa.gov`
- `ecfr.gov`
- `govinfo.gov`
- `congress.gov`
- official court sites

Allowed use:

- discovery
- authority validation
- reviewer packet support
- filing workflow support

### Secondary analysis

Examples:

- accounting journals
- major CPA firm analysis
- reputable tax commentary

Allowed use:

- discovery
- framing research questions
- helping Tina explain an idea internally

Not allowed use:

- final filing support by itself

### Community leads

Examples:

- Reddit
- X
- niche forums

Allowed use:

- discovery only

Not allowed use:

- final filing support
- direct movement into return values

### Low-trust leads

Examples:

- 4chan
- anonymous imageboards
- paste dumps

Allowed use:

- optional discovery only

Special rule:

- these sources should be quarantined as very low trust
- they may inspire a question, but they never count as support

## Tina's Four Decision Buckets

### 1. Authoritative and usable

Requirements:

- primary authority exists
- substantial authority exists
- no special disclosure path is required

Effect:

- Tina may move the idea into the filing workflow
- human review is still required

### 2. Usable with disclosure

Requirements:

- primary authority exists
- reasonable basis exists
- disclosure path is required
- not a prohibited shelter-style position

Effect:

- Tina may prepare the idea for review
- human approval is mandatory
- disclosure handling must be explicit

### 3. Interesting but unsupported

Requirements:

- idea looks potentially valuable
- support is incomplete or missing

Effect:

- Tina keeps the idea in the research queue
- it cannot change the return yet

### 4. Reject

Examples:

- frivolous position
- plainly unsupported position
- position supported only by low-trust chatter

Effect:

- Tina keeps it out of the return
- Tina does not spend more filing effort on it unless primary authority later changes the result

## Product Rules

- Tina may never cite Reddit, X, 4chan, or similar sites in a reviewer packet as filing authority.
- Tina may never let a community source directly change a filed number.
- Tina should label every tax idea with source class, risk, and current decision bucket.
- Tina should preserve the discovery trail so a reviewer can see where an idea came from.
- Tina should preserve the authority trail separately so a reviewer can see why the idea survived.

## UI Rule

When Tina presents a deep-cut idea to the owner or reviewer, she should say:

- where the idea came from
- whether it is just an idea or already supported
- what primary authority says
- whether it needs disclosure
- whether a human must decide

The user should never have to guess whether Tina is showing:

- a clever lead
- a vetted position
- or a bad idea that got rejected
